About CREAM

What it does

knowledge reigns supreme over nearly everyone.

A personal earnings ledger that turns year-by-year salary data into a forensic career picture — percentile rank vs all US earners, social-class mobility over time, inflation-adjusted purchasing power, personal cost-of-living vs official CPI, and pattern detection across decades of data. Local-first, single-file, no backend.

The 36 Chambers

twelve forensic insights, all on one panel.

Peak Percentile Power — exact salary needed today to reclaim your all-time percentile rank.

Class Mobility — years spent in each US income class with peak-class and 5-yr trend.

Percentile Velocity — average pts/yr movement vs peer earners.

Inflection Points — flags real-salary jumps (>15%) and stagnant runs (≥3yr flat) with job-change correlation.

Decade Ledger — nominal, real, avg percentile bucketed per decade.

Inflation Drag — lifetime purchasing power erased, plus worst "ghost raise" years.

Your COL vs Inflation — personal cost-of-living growth rate vs official CPI; gap tells you if your lifestyle inflates faster than the country.

Per-Job Summary, Salary Velocity, Job Transitions — tabular breakdown of every role you've held.

Old Money · Cumulative & Projection — sampled years in base-year dollars (with inflation loss flagged) plus a 5-yr regression forecast.

Data sources

the bills are real. so is the math.

Percentile thresholds — US individual income from Census / IRS SOI, 1993–2025, interpolated.

CPI / GDP — BLS CPI-U and BEA real GDP, year-over-year.

COL components — BLS rent CPI, food-at-home / food-away CPI, EIA gasoline retail prices, EIA residential electricity, BEA Home Value Index for owners.

Regional zones — 10 ZIP-prefix cost zones (housing/food/transport multipliers).

Quick tips

protect ya net worth.

⌘ Cop It regularly — grabs a JSON backup of everything. Drop It to restore on a new device.

⌘ Toggle COL overlay then flip its mode in Settings → COL between dollars and "% of salary" — the % view reveals if your lifestyle is winning or losing the race against your raises.

⌘ Click an event band on the chart to jump straight to the Events tab and edit it.

⌘ Click a job in the sidebar legend to highlight only that job's years on the chart; click again to deselect.

⌘ Esc closes the settings drawer and the mobile sidebar.
